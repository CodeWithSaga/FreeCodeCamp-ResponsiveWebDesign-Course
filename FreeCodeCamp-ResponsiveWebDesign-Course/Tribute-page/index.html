Ratan Naval Tata

He Was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist

He served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and he held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017.

Here's a time line of Rata Tata's life:

  • 1937- Born on December 28 in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, to Naval and Sooni Tata.
  • 1959- Graduated from Cornell University: Earned a degree in architecture and structural engineering.
  • 1961- Joins Tata Steel: Began his career on the shop floor of Tata Steel, where he learned about the manufacturing process.
  • 1971- Director of NELCO: Tasked with reviving National Radio & Electronics, marking his first leadership role within Tata Group.
  • 1981- Chairman of Tata Industries: Appointed chairman, driving diversification and modernization initiatives.
  • 1991- Chairman of Tata Sons: Took over as chairman, transforming Tata Group into a global powerhouse.
  • 2000- Tetley Acquisition: Tata Tea acquired Tetley, marking Tata’s first major global expansion.
  • 2007- Acquisition of Corus Steel: Made Tata Steel one of the largest steel producers worldwide.
  • 2008- Launch of Tata Nano: Launched the world’s most affordable car to provide affordable mobility.
  • 2012- Retired from Tata Sons, shifting his focus to Tata Trusts and philanthropy. Acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover: Tata Motors acquired the luxury brands, bolstering Tata's position in the global auto industry.
  • 2020: Contributed Rs 500 crore through Tata Trusts for pandemic relief.
  • 2024- Ratan Tata passed away, leaving behind an enduring legacy of ethical leadership, philanthropy, and industrial innovation.
